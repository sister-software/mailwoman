## Section notes

- **Two confirmed stale clusters, both CRF-related.** `crf-decoder.mdx`, `viterbi-and-bio-validity.mdx`, and (to a lesser degree) `bio-labels.mdx` all describe a "learned CRF transitions are coming" arc that `dual-loss-curvature-conflict.mdx` closed out — CRF training is permanently CE-only (`crf_loss_weight=0.0`), with only the frozen structural transition mask kept at inference. `dual-loss-curvature-conflict.mdx` is the accurate, current source; the other three need their CRF-roadmap language rewritten to point at it rather than promising a future retrain.
- **Shared-multilingual-model claim.** `neural-classification.mdx` states the shipped model was "trained on en-US + fr-FR data" and that "each new locale needs its own training run and its own weights package." Per project history the `neural-weights-fr-fr` npm package ships the en-us weights (soft-feed publish), so this claim needs verification/correction alongside the CRF fixes. This page is also explicitly called "superseded" by `how-the-model-reasons.mdx` in that page's own See Also — a second, independent signal it needs a rewrite pass rather than just a patch.
- **The README.mdx section index is badly out of date.** It was last touched 2026-06-08 and enumerates roughly 15 of the 50 pages now in this directory. Given the audience-routing goal of this whole cleanup (use / system / domain), this page is the highest-leverage rewrite target in the section — it's the reader's first stop and currently mis-describes the section's actual shape.
- **Version-number drift.** Several pages (`crf-decoder.mdx`, `street-supplement-architecture.mdx`, `training-pipeline.mdx`, `bio-labels.mdx`) reference an old `v0.x`/`v3.0.0` model-version scheme that doesn't obviously map onto the project's current versioning. This wasn't chased down page-by-page (out of scope for Phase 0), but a future rewrite pass should normalize or drop version numbers that no longer resolve to anything.
- **Merge candidate: the two FST prior pages.** `fst-gazetteer-prior.mdx` and `fst-priors-as-shallow-fusion.mdx` cover much of the same ground (admin FST, additive bias capped at 3.0, the Washington-DC-style disambiguation example) from two different entry angles. Recommend folding the shallow-fusion framing into `fst-gazetteer-prior.mdx` as an intro section and trimming `fst-priors-as-shallow-fusion.mdx` to what it uniquely covers (the speech-recognition analogy and the morphology-FST two-pass bias).
- _*The switching-from-* cluster (5 pages) is healthy but under-linked._* All five (libpostal, Nominatim, OpenCage, Pelias, Photon) are current, well-scoped integrator guides, consistently structured (one command, endpoint map, what's different, what's missing) and all point back to `how-mailwoman-compares.mdx`. Three of the five show zero inbound links from elsewhere, which is expected for a guide cluster reached mainly via search/comparison rather than cross-reference — not a defect, just worth noting they'd benefit from being surfaced as a named group in any new nav (e.g. an "Migrating from X" sidebar section) rather than five loose leaves.
- **Best product-reader entry paths, currently under-surfaced.** `what-mailwoman-is.mdx`, `how-close-is-close-enough.mdx`, `language-support.mdx`, `how-mailwoman-compares.mdx`, `confidence-calibration.mdx`, and `input-robustness.mdx` are the pages that most cleanly meet the "no ML background needed, statistical depth one link away" brief for the cartography-practitioner audience. Two of these (`how-close-is-close-enough.mdx`, `language-support.mdx`) have zero inbound links today — they read like they were written for a nav position that doesn't exist yet.
- **Misfiled / low-fit pages.** `deepseek-reasoning-budget.mdx` (an LLM API operational gotcha for corpus-generation workers) and `training-charts.mdx` (chart-tooling docs anchored to one specific v0.6.2 training comparison) don't serve any of the three reader intents this section is meant to route (use / system / domain) — they're maintainer-facing engineering notes that ended up in the public concept track. Recommend archiving both out of the default reader path.
